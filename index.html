About

I've spent 25 years closer to product decisions than most designers ever get.

At Acquisio, I held the PM title while we built Promote — a machine-learning ad platform for SMBs — from scratch. We owned the full funnel: ML-automated campaigns, conversion-optimized landing pages, and Twilio-powered call attribution that proved ROI to an audience that had been burned by agencies their whole lives. The product was acquired by Web.com. I've also designed systems that saved enterprise customers an estimated $140K per transaction cycle, unified fragmented product portfolios into coherent design systems, and written more Gherkin than most PMs will ever see.

I'm a product designer making the deliberate move to PM — not because design stopped mattering, but because I want to own the problem, not just the solution. I'm drawn to 0→1 and early-PMF environments where the lines between product, design, and strategy are still usefully blurry, and where being able to run discovery and open Figma and write a spec in the same afternoon is an asset rather than an anomaly.

Currently product designer at Negosh, a marketplace startup in brand licensing. Based in Montreal.
